Tales of an Adventurous Aquarius

Here, you can read about my endeavors in the GirlsWhoCode Summer Immersion Program!

Endeavors in SIP: Day 1

July 20, 2020

On the first day, I met my fellow classmates, TAs, and was introduced to html. Even though the zoom lesson was a little fast paced (understandable for a first day), it was very engaging. During the breakout sessions within the lesson, I got to meet some of my peers and interact with them on a personal level, which was fun. After the html lesson was over, I started the creation of my own website, but so far it's not that visually appealing haha

Endeavors in SIP: Day 2

July 21, 2020

On the second day, I worked on making my website more appealing by using CSS. In the beginning, I was figuring out how flexbox worked, but eventually I got the hang of it with the help of my TAs. For the breakout sessions, I bonded with my peers and TAs. It was nice to talk to one of the TAs about college stuff, since they were very informative. We also learned about each other's names through a sisterhood activity, and this allowed me to get to know my classmates on a deper level. Overall, the day went well and I'm excited for the Project Showcase and guest speakers tomorrow!

Endeavors in SIP: Day 3

July 22, 2020

Today, we shared our websites with others and explored JavaScript. This language allows us to add interactivity to the elements that come from HTML and CSS. HTML is basically the skeleton of the webpage, CSS is the styling of that skeleton, while JavaScript lets the user interact with the elements. After the lesson, we listened to our partner speaker, BlackRock, and learned about their efforts in sustainability. For today's work, we're creating our own personality quiz, so let's see how that goes!

Endeavors in SIP: Day 4

July 23, 2020

Today, we learned about the different events in Javascript. I added on to my personality quiz using Event Listeners in order to make my quiz more interactive. The DOM(Document Object Model) allows you to write code that interacts with the HTML page that loads in your browser. Basically, We also defined what functions and conditionals are in terms of computer science. For our sisterhood activity, we created our own poems, and I learned so much about my peers as we each shared what we wrote. It turns out, we have so much more in common than we think! The last thing I did today was embedding my personality quiz project onto my website. I would say that it was a success, but of course, there's lot of improvements to be made.

Endeavors in SIP: Day 5

July 24, 2020

Today was on the "chiller" side. We shared our personality quizzes with each other, and gave back productive feedback. We also discussed plans for the second week, and learned about our future project! I'm excited to use my newfound skills in order to raise awareness to a topic of my choice through the use of a website.
